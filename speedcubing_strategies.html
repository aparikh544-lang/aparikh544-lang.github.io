Finger Tricks

Finger tricks are essential for improving your cubing speed. They allow you to execute moves more quickly and efficiently. Here are some common finger tricks to practice:

  • R, R', L, and L' moves with your wrists (fairly easy)
  • U and U' moves with your index finger
  • F and F' moves with your index finger and thumb respectively
  • B and B' moves with your index or pinky finger
  • D and D' moves with your pinky finger

Of course these are only some of the finger tricks needed, but most finger tricks rely on the position of your hands and the cube.


Efficient Algorithms

Learning efficient algorithms can significantly reduce your solve times. Here are a few examples:

  • OLL (Orientation of the Last Layer) algorithms
  • PLL (Permutation of the Last Layer) algorithms
  • F2L (First Two Layers) algorithms
